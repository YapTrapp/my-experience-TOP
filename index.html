My Experience With VirtualBox and Why I Recommend Trying WSL2

Why the web page?

Hi guys! I'm new to The Odin Project and to Discord in general, so I didn't realize there was a character limit. I wanted to share my experience regardless in case it's helpful for anyone else, so I decided to make this little page that I can link without clogging up the feed. It also gives me a reason to practice "pushing" my files to my Github!

Why I Chose VirtualBox

I initially chose to use a VM because after reading through the options (VM, dual booting, or WSL2) VirtualBox sounded the least intimidating and most approachable to me. I was able to follow along with the installations lesson without any major hang ups or issues due to the excellent instructions provided by TOP, and I felt very accomplished and proud of myself the first time I opened up Firefox inside of my VM.

The Windows Woes

The night I successfully created my VM, I went to bed self satisfied and absolutely bursting with enthusiasm and determination. "I've got grit." I thought to myself, "There's no way I'm going to give up."

The next day, Windows updated.

At the same time, I was working on following the guide to disable Hyper V. (🐢) When I went to initialize my new, exciting VM, the initialization was aborted. I checked the log and couldn't identify any obvious errors (although that may be due to me lacking the ability to spot the error), so I began googling. First, I tried undoing everything I had just done to disable Hyper V with the thought that I must have accidentally messed something up in the process of attempting to disable it. Disregarding my virtualization settings, I was still getting the error and my VM was failing to initialize, so I asked Claude for ideas and Claude directed me to install the VirtualBox extension pack. That also didn't work, and I began getting a new USB proxy service error alongside failing to initialize. I checked the log again and I still wasn't seeing anything that I could identify as wrong, so I got frustrated and gave up for approximately two weeks.

Discovering WSL2

Once I finished sulking over the unfairness of it all, I went back to the installations lesson and read through my options again. I was nervous to try dual booting or WSL2 because it seemed like those options were above my head and I wasn't confident in my abilities to succeed. Once I made the decision to try WSL2, I was able to get my working environment up and running within minutes. So far, WSL2 has been intuitive and frictionless, and I've been able to dedicate my time to learning rather than troubleshooting.

Failing Forward

There's a concept I learned about in a class once that's been reverberating around my skull since learning about "grit."

It's called Failing Forward, by John C. Maxwell. The concept is essentially the mindset of seeing failure as an opportunity for growth rather than frustration or disappointment. While I did fail to get VirtualBox working in the end, I learned a lot about Hyper V, virtualization software, and kernel drivers in the process of failing that I otherwise wouldn't have learned. Thus, failing forward.

However, my ability to retroactively reflect and appreciate my failures does not negate my two week rage quit. My goal in creating this page is as practice for myself yes, but I also really want to encourage anyone having trouble with VirtualBox to try WSL2 before giving up. It might not click for everyone, but that doesn't mean it won't click for you. There's a reason why you're here, why you're trying to do this. If you believe that it's too hard or that you aren't smart enough to learn, I'm writing this for you. Fail forward.
